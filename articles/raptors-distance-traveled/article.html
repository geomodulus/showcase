On Halloween night, the Toronto Raptors beat Trae Young and his Atlanta Hawks 139–109 in a game that was over a good six or seven minutes before the final buzzer.

After the win, all-NBA forward Pascal Siakam gave a TV interview from the floor, having put up 30 points, 12 rebounds, and six assists in 35 minutes — a performance that was all the more important because the team was without all-star guard Fred VanVleet.

“We didn’t give the effort that we usually do last game,” he told Sportsnet’s Savanna Hamilton, referring to the Raptors’ Friday-night loss to the Philadelphia 76ers. “We just had to come out with energy.”

And come out with energy they did. The Raptors blew the doors off the Hawks, leading by as many as 35. They also ran hard from the opening tip to the tune of 43 fast-break points.

But have you ever wondered how far Siakam and his teammates actually traveled over the course of the game?

Thanks to NBA player-tracking software, we know the answer to that. On NBA.com, we can find the distance traveled and average speed for every player in every game. Here’s the data for the Oct. 31 Hawks game:

That said, it’s one thing to know Siakam covered TK km in the game, but it’s another thing to see it.

Like, what if Siakam hadn’t moved TK km around the Scotiabank Arena court — but instead left the building altogether and traveled that same distance through the city? Where would he get to? I mean, he wouldn’t have scored 30 points, and the team would have sorely missed him, but maybe he would have discovered something new.
