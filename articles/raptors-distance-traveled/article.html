On Nov. 9, the Toronto Raptors beat Jalen Green and his Houston Rockets 116–109 in a game at their home Scotiabank Arena that was close throughout.

All-start point guard Fred VanVleet had a game-high 32 points in 35 minutes — a performance that was all the more important because the team was without All-NBA third-team forward Pascal Siakam.

As is often the case, the Raptors ran hard, collecting 23 fast-break points to the Rockets’ 19.

But have you ever wondered how far VanVleet and his teammates actually traveled over the course of the game?

Thanks to NBA player-tracking software, we know the answer to that. On NBA.com, we can find the distance traveled for every player in just about every game. Here’s the data for the Nov. 9 Rockets contest:

Player Distance Travelled
O.G. Anunoby 3.7 km
Fred VanVleet 1.2 km
Scottie Barnes 4.4 km
Gary Trent Jr. 4.1 km
Thaddeus Young 0.6 km
Otto Porter Jr. 0.7 km
Christian Koloko 2.7 km
Chris Boucher 2.6 km
Precious Achiuwa 2.4 km
Dalano Banton 0.6 km

That said, it’s one thing to know Raptors All-Defensive Team candidate OG Anunoby covered TK km in the game, but ... how far is that really?

Like, what if — instead of moving TK km around the Scotiabank Arena court — — Anunoby left the building altogether and traveled that same distance through the city? Where would he get to?

I mean, he wouldn’t have tied a season-high with 27 points, and the team would have sorely missed him, but maybe he would have discovered something new.
