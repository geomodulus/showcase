On Halloween night, the Toronto Raptors beat/lost to Trae Young and his Atlanta Hawks in a game that went down to the final buzzer after 48 minutes of play.

As has become their custom as a team with one of the shortest rotations in the National Basketball Association, the Raptors’ starters played a huge proportion of the contest, each patrolling the floor for at least TK minutes.

After the game, all-star point guard Fred VanVleet gave a TV interview from the floor through gasps for air, having run up and down the hardwood dozens of times over his TK minutes played.

But have you ever wondered how far he and his teammates actually traveled over the course of that game?

Thanks to NBA player-tracking software, we know the answer to that. On NBA.com, we can find the distance traveled and average speed for every player in every game. Here’s the data for the Oct. 31 Hawks game:

Player Distance Travelled (in km)
Fred VanVleet 4.57
OG Anunoby 4.33
Gary Trent Jr. 4.02
Pascal Siakam 3.96
Scottie Barnes 3.88
Precious Achiuwa 2.22
Christian Koloko 1.98
Thaddeus Young 1.37
Dalano Banton 1.3
Juancho Hernangomez 0.92

That said, it’s one thing to know VanVleet covered 4.57 km in the game, but it’s another thing to see it.

Like, what if FVV hadn’t moved 4.57 km around the Scotiabank Arena court — but instead left the building altogether and traveled that same distance through the city? Where would he get to? I mean, he wouldn’t have scored TK points, and the team would have sorely missed him, but maybe he would have discovered something new.
